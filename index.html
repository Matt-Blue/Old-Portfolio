Too Many Tasks Manager


This task manager made with the Laravel PHP framework is a simple yet powerful priorities based task manager. Utilizing the MVC framework and baked in user registration, users can sign up and get started creating and organizing tasks by date and priority. The application is user-first, allowing for mobile use, multiple settings to customize the user's dashboard and an easy to use system for keeping track of day-to-day tasks.

Spanish


Sixth grade was the first time I was allowed to select an elective. Most of the guys wanted to take shop, most girls wanted to take home economics, me on the other hand, I wanted to learn something advanced that I could use in the real world; Spanish. Throughout Middle and High School I took Spanish every year, and this led up to my senior year.

I moved from Colorado to Florida during the Summer before my senior year, and upon entering my new high school experienced a large culture shock. In Colorado, Spanish class meant a handful of non-fluent speakers learning from a fluent teacher. In Florida, Spanish class meant a handful of fluent Spanish speakers, a fluent Spanish teacher, and myself. The culture in Florida is much more diverse and the latino culture specifically is very prominent. Despite this large culture shock, I persevered and learned much more culture and native dialect because of it.

Since high school I have taken Spanish almost every year, and have completed the requirements for my Spanish minor. Moving forward I hope to translate my skills to the professional world and utilize my abilities to bridge cultural and language barriers.

Vineyard Church


Every Wednesday from 5-10pm I went, rather with friends or alone, to the Vineyard Church to serve food to the less fortunate. The first task at hand was always to roll silverware and set up tables and chairs in preparation for dinner. Second we organized groceries into packages which would be distributed to the attendees after dinner. Third we were in charge of serving dinner, which was made by other volunteers, to the people who were already seated throughout the service area in the church. After serving dinner we always went out and sat with the people we served food to and tried to have quality conversations with them. During this time the pastor would do his sermon for the day as we ate and listened. Afterwards we went back into the kitchen and started to grab grocery bags for distribution. Once all the bags were given out, the work was done and all that was left to do was talk with all those still there at that point, play with the kids, and go home. Although it was a lot of work, the happiness of the kids as they played, the gratitude of the people we served, and the joy of the other volunteers made the experience very enjoyable and rewarding.



Road Cleanup


Every month, the brothers of my fraternity go out to SW 34th Street in Gainesville FL with orange jackets, reachers, and trash bags to go pick up trash on the side of the road. Every cleanup we fill up about two full trash bags with items that have been irresponsibly thrown out of the windows of cars onto the side of the road. Out in the sun every first Sunday of the month is not a fun job, neither is picking up bags of dog waste, beer bottles, and any other lovely items we happen to come across. It saddens me how much trash is just thrown onto the side of the road, but knowing I have other people that will help me better society gives me hope and drives me to keep giving back.



S.O.S. Smash and Shave to Save


Both of these events were projects that I led to raise money for S.O.S. Children's Villages. For S.O.S. The person with the most money put towards their name was to get their head shaved, and if we hit our goal all of us were to shave our heads. We did not reach our goal, however we still raised over $300 in one week.
